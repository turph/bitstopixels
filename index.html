What I Do

Designing experiences and products

research & testing

Every idea, product and feature should be able to withstand user validation. Finding the right time for, and balance of, qualitative and quantitative measurements validates development.

Analytics

Running tests and collecting data is great, but if you can not interpret the numbers it is of little value. Digging through Omniture and creating spreadsheets is the unglamorous UX work.

optimization

Lots of small incremental gains add up. There is a time to make bold bets and do big projects, but most times optimizing existing workflows and experiences lead to measurable gains.

design

The word "design" covers a lot of area these days - from esthetics, branding, patterns to UI. I consider myself a graphic designer, but my skills today are centered around building experiences.

My current role

Product discovery and personalization. Understanding how visitors search for products is fascinating. By grouping search term types and analyzing how groups of similar visitors progress through a site, I am experimenting with changing how; results are presented, products are clustered, and the appropriate time to allow filtering or sorting. Then again, perhaps visitors should not even have to search. I am also utilizing implicit and explicit visitor data, to experiment with reshaping entire shopping experiences which guide visitors towards categories, brands and products that should be of interest to them.
