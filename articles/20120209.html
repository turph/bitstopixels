Creating advertising campaigns

SellerCentral tool for creating a campaign

Tom Murphy Jan 09, 2012

title

sub

Problem

As ProductAds matured, so did merchant's advertising strategies. Merchants desired much more control over how advertising budgets were spent, and this required a complete shift in how the program worked. ProductAds needed to mature from merchants uploading a product feed into the Amazon catalog and relinquished all control over to Amazon targeting systems. Merchants desired a system similar to Google AdWords and Microsoft adCenter, one where they could create and manage complex advertising campaigns handling their spend and reach.

Solution

Campaign Manager was the tool which would be accessed from a merchant's SellerCentral account and would handle all aspects of creating and managing the various ProductAds programs; On Amazon as well as Off Amazon. The primary use case was to supply a workflow capable of a stepped approach to supply required parameters to create any type of advertising campaign campaign.

The second evolution of the ProductAds program was to empower merchants to not only rely on Amazon algorithms to display their product ads, but to create custom campaigns controlling which products could be shown, at what times and where. While this program was mainly intended for merchants looking to run advertisements off Amazon (considered syndication), the merchants would use this tool to also set up their traditional on Amazon product advertising.

The work below details some of the problem definition, consideration, wireframe and prototyping that went into shaping the application. The majority of my work was directional in nature - meant to inform internal discussions of the program and ultimately a Campaign Manager application incorporated into SellerCentral.

What's the end product?

This program has two masters; the merchant who wants as much control as possible in displaying their products and Amazon who wants to maximize clicks happening within the ad units. We realized from the start that while it may seem these are equal goals, there were differences. As a group we were having issues talking to each other about these differences so it was important to step back and understand what we thought the various ad units and placements where that constituted an advertising campaign.

This simple sketch of different widget types helped direct the conversation.

Vernacular of the ad unit

Once we had a clearer understanding of ad units we were trying to produce, we begin slicing them to understand what data the merchants needed to supply and our systems required to assemble the final product. Since we were operating in a space dominated by Google AdWords, and all our merchants participated in this program, we needed to mimic as much vocabulary to insure ease of use. This common language was important not only for UI labeling to decrease learning curves, but also so that merchants could easily alter data feeds.

While there are differences in how the two programs work, there is almost a complete overlap of elements required to construct an ad unit. Again, a simple diagram of how we anticipated ad unit slicing, element naming conventions and overall structure helped shape discussions.

Defining workflows - service models

Based on the vernacular above there are three main parts of every advertising campaign; The campaign itself which contains schedule and budget information, the Ad Group which defines a set of products eligible to display, and lastly the ads/products themselves. Therefore we needed to get an understanding of the elements, steps and outcome for doing the basics to Create, Edit and Delete a campaign.

Service models were created for basic flows, again to focus discussion and also to allow the engineers to scope. These models put structure to processes around campaign management and allowed for sketches and mockups to begin.

Conclusions

The planning and decision process hopefully was helped along by this work. It was an extremely long process, much longer than it should have been but in the end a management tool was built. That will be another post.
