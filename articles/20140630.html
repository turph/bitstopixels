Circular Navigation

Recommendations on Handheld PDPs

Tom Murphy June 30, 2014

Product Recommendations

Handheld devices

Problem

The initial launch of our handheld site omitted several elements from the Product Detail Page (PDP). A recommended product widget was one element - something that at the time which seemed like a low risk choice, but in hindsight had a huge impact on user behavior and site metrics.

Solution

Display a single Rich Relevance strategy of Customers Also Viewed recommendation widget gave us an opportunity not only display the most successful desktop strategy. For our most highly trafficked Product Detail pages this strategy consistently displayed relevant product results.

Base Handheld PDP Metrics

Product detail pages are extremely commerce focused and if the visitor does not like the displayed product their only option is to initiate a search or browse path from the main navigation (or exit). Our hypothesis was that introducing a recommended product widget would allow users to continue browsing with a simple one-click interaction without main navigation engagement. This circular navigation would allow the visitor stay within a similar product area of the catalog.

In user testing and site usage metrics we see much less engagement with search and browse elements on handheld sites. This is expected behavior as searching is difficult due to limitations of data entry, while browsing is challenging due to cumbersome navigation via multi level panels caused by catalog taxonomy issues.

MVP - People Also Viewed & stacked product results

The widget display is a simplistic layout, having 4 products stacked vertically. This layout removed any complex sliding or touch gestures, but did have issues of smaller product images and pushing other page elements further outside the viewport. Regardless, for the sake of testing, simplicity of implementation was paramount.

A/B test with widget added to page

Widget Engagement

The A/B test ran for 3 weeks and widget CTR was 8.23% compared to ~5% CTR for recommendation widgets on desktop. These numbers were encouraging for overall visitor engagement of the widget.

Main Navigation Usage

In order to understand if widget clicks were additional or if visitors were decreasing clicks on other elements, we tracked broad usage of main navigational elements; Menu, Home & Search.

Widget addition reduced main navigation usage for all three elements; Menu (-10.6%), Logo (-17.0%) and Search (-9.8%). This data shows that visitors are choosing to navigate by product opposed to engaging with the site's main navigation.

Bounce Rate

In order to understand if widget clicks were additional or if visitors were decreasing clicks on other elements, we tracked broad usage of main navigational elements; Menu, Home & Search.

Traffic segmented by mobile visitors saw bounce rates dropped significantly once the experiment was launched.

Conclusions

The recommendation widget greatly altered a visitor's behavior by decreasing navigation clicks and increasing on-click to product.
